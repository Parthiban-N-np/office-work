Aletta Jacobs (1854–1929) was a Dutch physician and women's suffrage activist.Jacobs strove throughout her life to change laws that limited women's access to equality, starting in 1883 with an unsuccessful court challenge and eventually achieving success 100 years ago today, on 18 September 1919, with the signing of a suffrage bill into law.
